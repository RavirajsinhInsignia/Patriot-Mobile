Avoid High Maritime and In-Flight Data Roaming Charges

Travelers often face unexpectedly high phone bills during cruises or in flights due to costly maritime and in-flight roaming charges. Here's why:

Maritime Roaming:

While on a cruise, your phone connects to a maritime network that uses satellite communication instead of land-based cell towers.

How to Avoid Expensive Charges:

  • Switch your phone to airplane mode to prevent automatic connections.
  • Use the ship's Wi-Fi for calls, messaging, and data, which is usually offered in affordable packages.

In-Flight Roaming:

Similarly, on a plane, your phone may connect to the airline's in-flight Wi-Fi or cellular service, which also uses satellite communication.

How to Avoid Expensive Charges:

  • Set your phone to airplane mode when the flight begins.
  • Most airlines offer Wi-Fi packages at a fixed rate, which is generally much cheaper than roaming charges.
